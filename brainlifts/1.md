## Anki Settings for MCAT: The Technical Optimization Guide - Charlie Groves: https://studycardsai.com/blog/anki-settings-for-mcat

The default Anki settings are designed for lifelong learning, but the MCAT is a high-stakes event with a fixed date.
Anki is not a "learn it" tool, but a "do not forget it" tool.
The goal is to build a mental operating system. You should use content review, videos, and practice problems to build understanding first.
a more effective set for students with a test date under three months is 5 20 2160 5760.
5 and 20: These are the initial "learning" phases. If you get it right, you see it in 5 minutes, then 20.
2160 and 5760: These are the "graduation" steps (36 hours and 96 hours). This ensures you can recall the fact after a sleep cycle and several days before it becomes a long-term review card.

If you are using pre-made MCAT decks, you may need shorter steps. Because you did not write the cards, you lack the initial context, making the first few exposures more difficult.

The "Interval Modifier" is a percentage that adjusts how quickly the intervals grow. The default is 100%. If you find that you are remembering too many cards too easily, you can increase this to 110% or 120%. This pushes the cards further into the future, reducing your daily workload. Conversely, if you feel you are forgetting cards just before they reappear, drop this to 90%.

SM-2 relies on a fixed "Ease" factor. If you hit "Hard" too often, you enter "Ease Hell," where the card appears every day regardless of whether you know it. This is a primary cause of Anki burnout for MCAT students.

The new FSRS (Free Spaced Repetition Scheduler) is a significant upgrade. It uses a mathematical model based on your actual performance history to predict the exact moment you will forget a card.

Before and after: Atomic transformation
Consider a student studying the kidney. A common mistake is to create a "paragraph card."

Bad Card (Compound):

    Front: Describe the function of the Juxtaglomerular (JG) apparatus.

    Back: The JG apparatus is located where the distal tubule meets the afferent arteriole. It senses sodium chloride levels via the macula densa and releases renin from JG cells to increase blood pressure via the RAAS pathway.

This card is a nightmare for the algorithm. If you forget only the location, but remember the renin part, do you mark it "Again" or "Good"? To fix this, split it into five atomic cards:

Good Cards (Atomic):

    Where is the JG apparatus located in the nephron? (Answer: Where distal tubule meets afferent arteriole)
    Which cells in the JG apparatus sense NaCl levels? (Answer: Macula densa)
    What hormone is released by the JG cells? (Answer: Renin)
    What is the primary goal of the RAAS pathway? (Answer: Increase blood pressure)
    The macula densa senses a decrease in what specific ion/molecule? (Answer: NaCl)

The ideal loop is: Content Review (Video/Book) → Practice Problems → Error Analysis → Anki Card Creation.

"What specific fact did I lack that caused me to miss this?" Create a card for that specific gap.

It is better to do 30 minutes of Anki every single day than to do a six-hour "hero session" once a week.
